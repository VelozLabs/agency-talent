## Content Formats

### Format 1: Scan Reveal (Highest Viral Potential)
**Duration**: 45–75 seconds
**Structure**:
1. Hook (0–3s): Stakes setup — "I'm about to scan this [trim] [year] F-150 before [person] buys it"
2. Plug in the adapter (3–8s): Quick visual of OBDLink in port
3. Scan running (8–20s): App interface on screen, progress animation
4. Results reveal (20–50s): Walk through key findings, DTCs explained in plain English
5. Verdict (50–60s): "Would I buy this truck? Here's my take."
6. CTA (60–75s): "Scan before you buy" / app link in bio

**Target**: 70%+ view completion rate (verdict is the payoff — people watch to find out)

### Format 2: DTC Education (Highest Education Value)
**Duration**: 30–60 seconds
**Structure**:
1. Hook (0–3s): Show the DTC code, state the stakes
2. Plain English translation (3–20s): What it means in human language
3. Context (20–40s): Is this serious? On which trims/years? What causes it?
4. Action step (40–55s): What to do next (check warranty, see dealer, monitor it)
5. Disclaimer (55–60s): "Always verify serious findings with a professional mechanic"

**Series concept**: "DTC of the Week" — weekly educational post on a single common F-150 DTC code

### Format 3: Warranty Win Story
**Duration**: 30–45 seconds
**Structure**:
1. Hook: "This F-150 owner used AI diagnostics to get [X] covered under warranty"
2. Setup: What the DTC was, what the dealer said
3. TruckPulse match: AI matched it to a known TSB
4. Outcome: Covered, saved $X
5. Lesson: "Know your rights before you walk into the dealer"

**Target**: High save rate (practical value people want to reference later)

### Format 4: Quick Tip (Highest Post Frequency)
**Duration**: 15–30 seconds
**Structure**:
1. Hook: "Quick F-150 tip:"
2. Single insight: One fact, one action, one takeaway
3. CTA: "Follow for more"

**Example topics**: Best time to run a scan (cold start), how to interpret oil life vs. oil condition, what a pending DTC means vs. confirmed

## Cross-Platform Repurposing

Every TikTok video is repurposed for:
- **Instagram Reels**: Same video, slightly modified caption, different hashtag set
- **YouTube Shorts**: Same video, optimized thumbnail, YouTube SEO title
- **Reddit**: Scan reveal videos posted to r/f150 and r/FordTrucks as comments ("made a short video explaining this DTC that came up a lot in this sub")

**Production note** (Short Video Coach input):
- Shoot vertically (9:16) at minimum 1080p
- Text overlays on all key data points — many users watch without sound
- Captions/subtitles on every video (accessibility + muted viewing)
- Hook text on screen in first 3 frames (reinforces verbal hook)
- OBD-II app UI must be visible and legible on mobile screens — use screen recording with zoom

---

## Compliance & Disclaimer Integration

Every diagnostic content video includes one of:
- On-screen text overlay: "For informational purposes. Verify serious findings with a certified mechanic."
- Verbal disclaimer at end of video
- Caption disclaimer: "This is educational content, not professional mechanical advice."

**Non-negotiable**: Any video involving a high-severity DTC (P0300 misfire, P0420 catalyst, HVAC/PCM codes) must include both on-screen text AND verbal disclaimer.
