## Deal Strategist Analysis

### Monetization Model Assessment

**Recommended model**: Self-serve SaaS subscription with tiered pricing.

This is NOT an enterprise sales motion. The target buyer (owner-operator, 1-20 employees) makes fast purchasing decisions based on clear ROI, not committee approval. The buying cycle is:

```
Pain trigger → Google search / app marketplace browse → Landing page / listing
→ Free trial → First recovered invoice → "This pays for itself" → Convert
```

**Average deal cycle**: 14-21 days (trial to paid). No demo calls needed. No sales team required at launch.

### Pricing Strategy Validation

| Tier | Price | Target | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | $29/mo | Solo operators, <30 invoices/mo | QB integration, email follow-ups, 3-step sequences, basic dashboard |
| **Growth** | $49/mo | Small teams, <100 invoices/mo | + SMS follow-ups, cash-flow view, per-customer tone settings, priority support |
| **Accountant** | $99/mo | Bookkeepers managing multiple clients | Multi-client dashboard, bulk sequence management, client-level reporting |
| **Annual discount** | 20% off | All tiers | $278/yr, $470/yr, $950/yr — improves cash flow and reduces churn |

**Pricing rationale**:
- **$29 floor** anchored to "one recovered invoice pays for a year" framing. Average B2B invoice for micro-businesses: $500-3,000. Even one payment recovered 5 days faster has tangible value.
- **$49 ceiling** for core product keeps it impulse-buy territory. No procurement process needed.
- **$99 accountant tier** is a leverage play: one accountant = 10-50 SMB clients. Higher willingness to pay because the tool directly serves their client advisory role.

### Willingness-to-Pay Assessment

**Strong indicators**:
1. **Direct dollar attribution** — Unlike productivity tools or analytics, this product can say "we helped you collect $X faster." That's the strongest WTP anchor possible.
2. **Pain is recurring** — Late payments aren't a one-time problem. Every month brings new invoices and new follow-up needs. Subscription fatigue doesn't apply when the tool actively generates returns.
3. **Low switching cost from status quo** — Status quo is "doing it manually." There's no incumbent tool to displace for most target users, so there's no switching cost — only adoption cost.
4. **Accountant endorsement** — If accountants recommend it, the trust barrier drops to near-zero.

**Risk factors**:
1. **Free alternative perception** — "I can just send emails myself" is the biggest conversion objection. Must demonstrate that consistency + timing + tone >> manual effort.
2. **Subscription fatigue** — SMBs already pay for QB/Xero, email, phone, etc. Another $29-49/mo needs to clear the "is this worth it?" bar every month.

## Growth Hacker Analysis

### Customer Acquisition Cost Estimates

| Channel | Estimated CAC | Volume Potential | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| QB/Xero Marketplace (organic) | $15-25 | High | Lowest CAC; listing optimization is key |
| SEO / Content | $30-50 | Medium-High | Ramp time 3-6 months; compounds over time |
| Referral (user → user) | $10-20 | Medium | "You recovered $X — share with a friend" |
| Accountant referral | $20-40 | High (per referral = multiple clients) | Rev share: 20% of first year |
| Google Ads | $80-120 | Medium | Expensive but immediate; use for validation only |
| Community seeding | $0-15 | Low-Medium | Time-intensive; authentic presence required |

**Blended CAC target**: $40-60 at scale. Achievable if marketplace + organic + referral dominate the mix.

### Conversion Funnel Design

```
AWARENESS (Marketplace listing, SEO content, referral)
    │ Target: 10,000 monthly impressions by month 6
    ▼
CONSIDERATION (Landing page, marketplace detail page)
    │ Target: 15% click-to-trial rate
    ▼
TRIAL START (Connect QuickBooks, see "Who Owes You" screen)
    │ Target: 60% complete onboarding (connect QB + view invoices)
    ▼
ACTIVATION (First automated reminder sent)
    │ Target: 40% of trials send first reminder within 48 hours
    ▼
CONVERSION (Trial → Paid)
    │ Target: 15-25% trial-to-paid at day 14
    ▼
RETENTION (Monthly renewal)
    │ Target: 94-97% monthly retention (3-6% churn)
    ▼
EXPANSION (Starter → Growth, referrals)
    │ Target: 10% upgrade rate; 15% referral rate
```

### Key Metrics to Track from Day 1

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Time to first reminder sent** | <1 hour from signup | Activation speed predicts conversion |
| **Trial-to-paid conversion** | >15% | Below 10% signals product or pricing problem |
| **Month 3 retention** | >85% | Validates ongoing value beyond initial recovery spike |
| **Recovered $ per user per month** | >$500 | Proves the "pays for itself" narrative |
| **Net Promoter Score** | >40 | Referral engine requires promoters |
| **CAC payback period** | <3 months | Sustainable growth |
