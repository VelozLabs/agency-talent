## Gate Criteria

Gates are checkpoints where the team pauses for human review before proceeding. A gate blocks the next session until it's explicitly cleared.

### Gate 02 — Reddit Strategy Approval
Before proceeding to TikTok:
- [ ] Subreddit targeting list confirmed (primary + secondary communities)
- [ ] 90/10 content ratio strategy accepted
- [ ] Karma-building timeline is realistic (3–6 month runway)
- [ ] AMA strategy has an identified subject matter expert

### Gate 03 — TikTok Strategy Approval
Before building content calendar:
- [ ] Content pillars (40/30/20/10 mix) agreed upon
- [ ] Hook formulas reviewed and approved
- [ ] Creator partnership tier strategy confirmed (budget available?)
- [ ] Cross-platform repurposing workflow validated

### Gate 05 — Paid Amplification Budget Sign-Off
Before any paid spend:
- [ ] Monthly budget ceiling approved
- [ ] Reddit Promoted Posts vs. TikTok Ads allocation confirmed
- [ ] Creative testing framework reviewed (minimum 3 variants per campaign)
- [ ] Attribution model agreed upon before launch

### Gate 07 — Final Verdict (Orchestrator)
Before full execution:
- [ ] Reality Checker score ≥ 7/10
- [ ] No critical blockers identified
- [ ] Resource requirements are funded
- [ ] 90-day roadmap signed off

## Team Constraints

- No promotional-first Reddit content — value-first always (90/10 rule)
- TikTok hooks must be validated within 24 hours of posting; low-completion videos get pulled
- All paid campaigns require creative pre-testing before scaling budget
- Orchestrator has final veto on any strategy that conflicts with brand voice
