## Part 0 — Orchestrator Synthesis Notes

**Wave sequencing worked as intended.** Name was locked in Wave 1 before any visual, content, product, or community work began. All downstream agents built on a consistent foundation.

**Cross-agent consistency is strong.** Brand pillars (Show Up Culture · Bilingual by Default · Community Before Commerce · Unapologetically Dad) appear consistently in voice, content, product, and community architecture. No agent contradicted another on cultural standards.

**One open decision flagged (see Part 7):** Creative Director specified Charcoal crown + Burnt Orange undervisor as the founding cap colorway; Merch Developer specified Forest Green + Khaki for El Casquete. ## Part 1 — Brand Foundation

> Full detail: `parts/01-brand-foundation.md`

### Brand Name
**SIDELINE PAPI** — "Sideline" places the wearer physically at the field. "Papi" is a cultural identity marker, not a translation. Six characters on a brim. Handle @SidelinePapi is clean and searchable.

Eliminated: Touchline Dads (skews Anglo), The 90th Minute (names a moment not a person), Pitch Dads Co. (generic), Primera Fila (fails bilingual test).

### Tagline
**"Every Saturday, we show up."** Operates simultaneously as behavioral description, community declaration, and emotional statement about presence as a choice.

### Positioning Statement
For dads and dad figures who spend weekends at youth soccer fields — especially in bilingual and bicultural Latino communities — Sideline Papi is the lifestyle and apparel brand that makes you feel seen, celebrated, and connected to a community of fathers who show up the same way you do.

### Brand Pillars
1. **Show Up Culture** — Presence is the brand's core value. Real dads, real attendance records, real names.
2. **Bilingual by Default** — The brand lives in both English and Spanish simultaneously. No "también en español" addenda.
3. **Community Before Commerce** — Every commercial decision is tested against whether it strengthens the community.
4. **Unapologetically Dad** — Being a present, committed, sideline dad is worth celebrating at full volume. No "bad dad" humor.

### Voice
**Warm (not soft) · Confident (not chest-puffing) · Playful (not performative)**

Sounds like: *"Six AM. Cleats in the backseat. Coffee in your hand. You've been ready since Thursday. Sideline Papi."*

Never sounds like: *"Hey soccer fam! Feeling pumped? Drop a ⚽ if you're ready to CRUSH IT!"*

## Part 2 — Cultural & Bilingual Framework

> Full detail: `parts/02-cultural-framework.md`

### Language Mix by Platform

| Platform | Spanglish | English-dominant | Spanish-dominant |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 50% | 30% | 20% |
| TikTok | 60% | 25% | 15% |
| Email (Collective) | Segment-specific | Per preference | Per preference |

### Binding Language Rules
1. Code-switch at emotional seams, not grammatical boundaries
2. Never translate what you just said
3. Respect the grammar of each language inside the switch
4. Spanish appears at moments of depth, intimacy, and emotion; English dominates humor and logistics
5. Never use Spanish as decoration — use it because it is the better sentence

### Cultural Calendar (Key Dates)
- **Día del Padre / Father's Day** (June 15, 2026) — Drop 2 anchor; the brand's highest-visibility moment
- **Back-to-Season** (late August) — El Primer Partido ritual launch
- **Día de los Muertos** (Nov 1–2) — Reflection content, no commercial activation
- **Season close** (May/November) — Community retrospective, no product push

### Representation Standards
- No stock imagery; real dads from real fields
- Age range: 28–55 across all content
- Body diversity is default, not campaign
- Generational content includes abuelos as "dad figures"
- Neighborhood-specific content (not generic "Latino community")

## Part 3 — Visual Identity

> Full detail: `parts/03-visual-identity.md`

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Forest Green | #2D5016 | Primary brand color; trustworthy, outdoors, presence |
| Warm Cream | #F5F0E8 | Primary background; approachable, inclusive |
| Burnt Orange | #C65D2E | Accent; energy, warmth without aggression |
| Charcoal | #1C1C1C | Near-black for type and structure |
| Field Gold | #D4A843 | Limited use; community moments |

### Typography
- **Primary Display**: Freight Display Pro — editorial, confident, bilingual-ready
- **Secondary/Body**: Proxima Nova — clean, accessible, works at small sizes
- **Bilingual rule**: Both languages set in the same typeface at the same weight; neither is visually subordinate

### Logo System
- **Primary**: Wordmark — SIDELINE PAPI in small caps, tracked, Charcoal or Cream
- **Secondary**: Crest lockup — shield form with wordmark and a single soccer field line element
- **Mark only**: SP monogram for embroidery and small applications
- **Never**: Drop shadows, gradients, script fonts, clip art soccer balls

### Photography Brief
- **Time of day**: Golden hour morning (6:30–9:00 AM) or late afternoon (4:00–6:30 PM)
- **Subject**: Dads on the sideline, not players on the field
- **Color treatment**: Natural, slightly warm — no cold filters, no heavy contrast
- **Never**: Studio setups, white-background product shots, models who do not look like the community

## Part 6 — Community Architecture

> Full detail: `parts/06-community-architecture.md`

### The Collective

**What it is**: The inner community — not a mailing list. Members heard about Sideline Papi before there was anything to buy.

**Five tangible member benefits**:
1. **48-Hour Founding Window** on every drop (cart access before public launch)
2. **The Thursday Dispatch** — every other Thursday, one question, no product, real reply inbox
3. **Chapter Invitation Access** — 7 days early notification when a chapter forms near them
4. **Founding Member Designation** — permanent for anyone who joins before Drop 1 goes public; logged, never retroactively awarded
5. **Direct Product Feedback Line** — one open-ended feedback request per drop cycle, read by founding team, acknowledged with follow-up

**Segmentation**: Spanglish-First · English-Primary · Engaged Core (2 of last 4 emails) · Dormant (90+ days) · Purchasers

**List health target**: 50–65% Engaged Core of total list. Protect engagement rate, not list size.

### Local Chapter Model

**Structure**: Chapter lead + 8 active members minimum. Lead is field contact, ~30 min/week. Not a manager, not an influencer.

**What chapters get**: City identity kit · WhatsApp group · welcome cards · sideline banners · direct brand contact · monthly 15-min call (first 90 days) · annual field kit (sunscreen, branded trash bags, handwritten note)

**What the brand gets**: Real-field photography · market intelligence · word-of-mouth acquisition · proof of concept for press and retail

### Chapter Expansion Sequence

1. **Los Angeles** (East LA / San Gabriel Valley) — founding market
2. **Houston** (Harris County / Pasadena) — Month 4–5
3. **Chicago** (Pilsen / Little Village) — Month 5–6
4. **Dallas–Fort Worth** (Irving / Grand Prairie) — Month 6–8
5. **Phoenix** (Maryvale) — Month 8–10; first to get local press coverage

**Trigger for each new city**: (1) founding chapter has 2+ events and 8+ active members AND (2) 3+ dads in the new city have independently requested a chapter AND (3) chapter lead confirmed via phone/voice call (not a form).

### Week 6 Chapter Event — The Unmistakable Moment

90-minute run-of-show. No stage. No PA. No banner unless the lead wants it.

The moment that is unmistakably Sideline Papi and nothing else: **7:38 AM — The Circle**. Chapter lead asks one question: *"¿Cuándo fue la primera vez que fuiste el papá en la banda — y qué se sintió? / When was the first time you were the dad on the sideline — and what did it feel like?"*

Dads standing in a circle at a field, coffee getting cold, kids in the background, no product in sight. That silence before the first dad answers is the brand.

### Community Health Metrics

| Metric | Healthy | Warning |
|---|---|---|
| Thursday Dispatch reply rate | 8–15% | Below 4% (2 consecutive) |
| Chapter attendance (60-day) | 60–75% | Below 40% (2 consecutive) |
| Content permission rate | 100% | Any undocumented use |
| Pre-drop Collective conversion | 12–20% | Below 7% |
| First-name recognition at events | 100% founding / 80%+ established | Below 70% |
| Engaged Core % of total list | 50–65% | Below 35% |

### 90-Day Launch Calendar Summary

| Week | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 1 | Private conversations with 12–15 founding dads (no public presence) |
| 2 | @SidelinePapi goes live; founding dad portrait; Klaviyo capture live |

**Day 90 targets**: 400 Collective members · Chapter 1 = 2 events · Drop 1 = 60%+ units sold

### Risks

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| R-1 | **List without community** — Collective grows to 400 but no one replies to Thursday Dispatches | Medium | Ship the first Thursday Dispatch before Drop 1; reply rate is the leading indicator, not list size |
| R-2 | **Chapter lead burnout** — founding chapter lead drops out before Month 3 | Medium | Identify a co-lead at Week 6 event; chapter lead should never be a single point of failure |
| R-3 | **Drop 1 undersells** — fewer than 60% of units move in the Collective window | Low if community work is done | Do not launch Drop 1 until Thursday Dispatch reply rate is 8%+; commerce follows belonging |
| R-4 | **Cultural inauthenticity signal** — community detects performative bilingualism | Low given framework | All copy reviewed against cultural framework binding rules before publish; Spanish used because it's better, not for optics |
| R-5 | **Permission gap** — photo used without documented individual consent | Low with process | Content permission rate is a 100% metric; any deviation is treated as a process failure, not an edge case |
