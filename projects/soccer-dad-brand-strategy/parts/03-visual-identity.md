## 1. LOGO DIRECTION

### Primary Direction: B — Crest/Badge

**Selected for Phase 1 launch.**

The Sideline Papi crest is a flattened shield — not a European football club shield, not a coat of arms. Think of it as a sideline credential badge crossed with a varsity letter patch: the kind of thing that lives on a jacket chest or a stadium seat armrest. The overall silhouette is slightly wider than it is tall, with a flat top edge and a soft pointed bottom. No ornate flourishes. No laurels. No banner ribbons unless they serve a specific purpose (see chapter badge application below).

**Inside the shield, from top to bottom:**

- A thin top band contains "EST. 2025" in tight, condensed sans-serif caps — the founding year anchors the brand as intentional, not trend-chasing.
- "SIDELINE" sits in the primary position: all-caps, bold condensed sans-serif (Dharma Gothic E, Heavy weight), tracking set slightly open at +20. It reads like a stadium scoreboard — authoritative, wide, unmistakable.
- "PAPI" sits directly below in a contrasting treatment: same condensed family but at approximately 140% the cap height of "SIDELINE," shifted slightly warmer by being set in the primary accent color (Burnt Orange, see palette). The size relationship inverts typical hierarchy — "PAPI" commands more visual weight because it is the cultural heartbeat of the name. Designers should read this as: SIDELINE tells you where we are; PAPI tells you who we are.
- A single thin horizontal rule separates the two words — not a decorative swash, just a 1pt divider in Off-White or Cream.
- Below "PAPI," a minimal pitch line element: a curved arc suggesting the top of a center circle, rendered in a single stroke, no fill. This is the only explicit soccer reference in the primary mark. It is subtle enough that someone unfamiliar with soccer reads it as a decorative base element; someone who has stood on a sideline for twelve weekends reads it immediately.

**At hat-brim scale (3 inches wide, one continuous row):**
The shield silhouette compresses cleanly. "SIDELINE PAPI" renders as two stacked lines within the badge without loss of legibility because both words are condensed. The "EST. 2025" micro-band may drop at this scale — the designer should produce a hat-specific lockup that removes the founding year band and tightens the interior spacing, keeping the pitch arc. The result reads as a complete credential in a single glance.

**At profile photo scale (150×150px circle):**
The shield sits centered with approximately 12px of negative space on all sides. At this size, "SIDELINE" and "PAPI" must remain legible — this is non-negotiable and is why the condensed-heavy weight was specified. The pitch arc drops to a suggestion. The designer must deliver a dedicated social avatar crop as part of the logo package (see asset checklist), not expect the full crest to resize automatically.

---

### Secondary Direction: C — Hand-Drawn Script

**For alternate applications: patches, chapter badges, embroidery on back panels, inside hang tags.**

A warm, unhurried script rendering of "Sideline Papi" as a single connected phrase — not casual to the point of sloppy, but written the way a dad would sign his kid's permission slip: with confidence, not with calligraphy. The script should feel like it was drawn with a medium-weight marker, not a fine-point pen. Strokes are consistent in weight, with minimal contrast between thick and thin. Letters connect naturally. No exaggerated swashes on the P or the S.

This secondary mark lives on the interior label of every garment and on back-panel embroidery on the founding hoodie. It reads as the personal, intimate counterpart to the bold crest. When someone sees the crest, they know the brand. When someone finds the script on the inside of their collar, they feel like the brand knows them.

For chapter badge applications: the script lockup sits inside a circular border with chapter city and state ("DALLAS · TX") running the bottom arc. This is the Direction B shield's secondary-use companion — the circle form reads as chapter-specific while the primary shield reads as the brand authority.

---

### Direction NOT Recommended: A — Wordmark with Subtle Soccer Element

**Direction A is not recommended for Phase 1 launch.** A standalone wordmark requires the letterforms themselves to carry brand personality with minimal visual support — this works when a brand name has strong inherent visual character (short, punchy, single word) or when the brand has earned enough recognition that the wordmark alone triggers recall. "Sideline Papi" is a two-word name launching to a cold audience. The name is memorable and culturally resonant, but a flat wordmark at launch will read as generic activewear before the brand has had time to build visual equity. The crest gives the mark a sense of belonging — like it has existed for seasons, like you might have missed it — which is exactly the emotion this brand needs to manufacture at launch.

## 2. COLOR PALETTE

### Primary Palette: "Saturday Morning"

**Selected: Option B — Warm Cream (#F2E8D9) · Burnt Orange (#C65D2E) · Forest Green (#2C4A2E) · Charcoal (#1C1C1C)**

**Palette name: Saturday Morning.**

Named for the specific quality of light at 7:45 AM on a youth soccer Saturday — the coffee is still warm, the field is still damp, the temperature hasn't decided what it wants to be yet. That light is cream and orange and deep green. It doesn't feel like a sports brand. It feels like a morning worth showing up for.

**Why this palette matches the brand at launch:**

- *Show Up Culture*: The palette is warm without being soft. Burnt Orange is a color that shows up — it reads from thirty feet away on a sideline. It signals presence without screaming.
- *Bilingual by Default*: Option C's navy-and-gold would read as European football club heritage. Option A's pitch green and black reads as performance athletic. Saturday Morning reads as neither — it reads as cultural, which is exactly where the bilingual-by-default pillar lives. It borrows from Mexican craft tradition (terracotta, cream, deep green) without being a costume.
- *Community Before Commerce*: Warm Cream as a dominant field color makes the brand feel like it was designed for a home, not a store. It is the color of a well-worn wall, a family photo, a notebook someone carries to every game.
- *Unapologetically Dad*: Burnt Orange is a dad color. Not in an ironic way — in the way that dads have been wearing it since 1987 without apology and have been right the whole time.

**Product color assignments:**

- **Founding Hat**: Charcoal (#1C1C1C) crown, Burnt Orange (#C65D2E) undervisor. Crest embroidered in Warm Cream. This is the anchor SKU — it needs to read as serious and wearable daily, not as a costume piece.
- **Founding Tee**: Warm Cream (#F2E8D9) body, Charcoal crest print. Single-color print. The tee should feel like it has been washed fifteen times already.
- **Hoodie**: Forest Green (#2C4A2E) body, Warm Cream drawstrings, Burnt Orange interior lining on hood. Crest embroidered in Warm Cream on chest left. Script embroidered in Warm Cream on interior collar.
- **Sticker Pack Background**: Each sticker sits on either Warm Cream or Charcoal. No sticker should be produced in pure white — use Warm Cream. One sticker in the founding pack uses Burnt Orange as the primary field color with Charcoal type.

**Prohibited combination:**

**Burnt Orange on Forest Green.** These two colors fight at this specific saturation level — the warm-cool contrast creates a visual vibration that reads as disorganized at small scale and aggressive at large scale. The brand voice is confident, not combative. This combination is not permitted on any product, social graphic, or print asset. If green and orange must coexist on a layout, Charcoal or Warm Cream must mediate between them.

---

### Secondary "Clean" Palette: Option A (Renamed "Pitch Week")

**For versatile/clean applications: email templates, website, document headers, media kits, any context where Saturday Morning reads as too warm or too casual.**

Black (#1A1A1A) · White (#FFFFFF) · Pitch Green (#2D6A4F) · Off-White (#F5F5F0). This is the brand in a collared shirt — same person, same confidence, slightly more structured context.

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Selected Pairing: Dharma Gothic E (Headline) + Nunito (Body) + Libre Franklin (Supporting/Accent)

---

**Headline: Dharma Gothic E, Heavy or ExtraBold weight**
Dharma Gothic E is condensed, powerful, and reads like it was designed to be stamped on the side of something permanent — it carries the physicality of the sideline without borrowing from athletic-brand cliché, because it reads as human scale rather than stadium scale.

**Body: Nunito, Regular and SemiBold**
Nunito has a warmth baked into its rounded terminals that matches the brand's "warm, not soft" voice — it is legible at caption size, approachable in paragraph form, and does not compete with the headline's weight.

**Supporting/Accent: Libre Franklin, Medium Italic**
Libre Franklin Italic at medium weight handles bilingual moments, secondary callouts, and the moments where Spanish appears at emotional peaks — it has enough personality to signal a register shift without breaking from the system. It is used sparingly: never as body copy, always as punctuation.

---

### Typeset Examples

**1. Instagram Feed Post**

```
DHARMA GOTHIC E HEAVY / 72pt / ALL CAPS / tracking +10
Every Saturday,

DHARMA GOTHIC E HEAVY / 72pt / ALL CAPS / Burnt Orange
WE SHOW UP.

NUNITO SEMIBOLD / 14pt / Charcoal / tracking +5 / centered beneath
Sideline Papi  ·  @sidelinepapi
```

Visual hierarchy: the headline fills 80% of the frame. The tagline ends with a period — not punctuation as an afterthought, but as a statement that the sentence is complete and the matter is settled. The handle sits at the bottom as a small, confident footer. No decorative borders. No drop shadows.

**2. Hat Back-Strap or Inside Label**

```
DHARMA GOTHIC E HEAVY / 8pt or embroidery equivalent
SIDELINE PAPI
EST. 2025
```

Three words, two lines, one small logo element if space permits. This is the mark that a dad reads when he flips his hat over to check the size and instead finds the brand looking back at him. It should feel like a signature, not a legal disclaimer. On the back strap: white on black webbing. On the interior label: printed in Charcoal on Warm Cream woven label.

**3. Email Header**

```
[Crest mark, centered, 80px tall]
DHARMA GOTHIC E HEAVY / 28pt / Charcoal / tracking +15
SIDELINE PAPI
NUNITO REGULAR / 11pt / Charcoal 60% opacity / tracking +30 / centered
Every Saturday, we show up.
```

The email header is not a logo dump. The crest sits above the wordmark as a beacon. The tagline sits below at reduced opacity — present, but not competing. Total header block should not exceed 160px in height. Background: Warm Cream (#F2E8D9). There is no hero image in the email header. The brand mark is the hero.

---

### Legibility Concerns at Embroidery Scale

**Dharma Gothic E at any size below 0.4 inches cap height will begin to lose interior letterform detail** — the crossbar of the E and the counter of the P compress first. For embroidery applications under 0.5 inches (such as the interior label), the designer must supply a simplified letterform set with slightly opened counters. The production embroidery team should receive a specific embroidery-optimized file, not the standard vector export.

**Nunito's rounded terminals are a liability below 6pt or below 0.25 inch embroidered.** Do not use Nunito for embroidery. On any embroidered application, substitute Libre Franklin Medium, which has more open apertures and survives stitch translation better.

**The pitch arc in the crest becomes a single stitch line below 1-inch badge diameter.** At that scale, drop the arc entirely and deliver the wordmark-only lockup.

## 4. PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION — First Content Shoot Brief

**This brief is to be handed directly to the photographer and casting director. No follow-up call should be necessary.**

---

### Setting

All content from the founding shoot takes place at a real youth soccer complex — not a park with open grass, not a turf rental facility with white walls, not a stadium. Specifically: a multi-field complex with visible sidelines, temporary or permanent bleachers, corner flags, and the ambient infrastructure of organized youth soccer (field numbers painted on, coaching boards, nets with visible wear). The Houston or Dallas metro area is preferred given target community density, but any comparable facility with authentic sideline infrastructure is acceptable.

**Time of day**: 7:30 AM to 10:30 AM only. This is non-negotiable. The quality of morning light at this hour — low angle, warm, slightly hazy in humid markets — is what creates the "Saturday morning" visual signature. No afternoon light. No golden hour (too romanticized for this brand).

**Weather**: Overcast is preferred over full sun. Overcast creates even, warm fill that flatters all skin tones and removes the need for reflectors that disrupt the candid register. Full sun is acceptable if the time window is respected. Rain reschedule — puddles and mud read as adversity, not presence.

**Indoor/Outdoor**: Outdoor only for all hero shots. Concession stand area may be used for secondary "pre-game" moments (ordering coffee, reading the bracket) but the field is always visible in the background.

---

### Talent

**Age range of dads**: 28–52. The range should be visible across the shoot — not every talent in the same age bracket. At least one dad should clearly read as 45+.

**Number of people per shot**: Most shots should feature 2–4 people in frame. A minimum of two shots must be of a single dad, not a group. These solo shots carry the most emotional weight and are used for founding campaign copy.

**Kids**: Present in approximately half the shots, but not the subject. Kids are mid-distance or background — visible in the story, not performing for the camera. No shot should be composed to make the child the focal point; the dad's presence and attention is always the story.

**Cultural casting requirement**: Per locked cultural framework, 60%+ of subjects must visibly be Latino or of Latin American descent. The casting director should source accordingly. This is a demographic and cultural accuracy requirement, not a tokenism directive — this community is the primary audience, and they will know immediately if the casting is performative.

**Clothing direction for talent**: Talent wears Sideline Papi founding gear mixed with their own clothing. The ratio should feel like real life — a dad in a Sideline Papi hat and his own jeans and a jacket he's had for six years. Not a full outfit pull. No talent should be head-to-toe branded. The gear is part of their wardrobe, not a uniform.

---

### Props and Wardrobe

**Dads are wearing**: Founding hat (required on at least one subject per shot), founding tee or hoodie depending on weather, their own pants — dark wash jeans, joggers, or athletic pants. No athletic shorts unless the shot specifically calls for it. No brand-new clean sneakers — this is game day, not a product launch event.

**Field gear present in at least half of shots**: Low camp chairs (the kind that fold into a bag), a branded or generic insulated coffee cup (not a logo'd corporate tumbler — a plain Hydro Flask or a café cup), a soccer ball that has visible game wear, a league schedule printed on paper (not a phone).

**Gear NOT present**: Branded sideline tents, sponsor banners, expensive camera equipment, matching parent group t-shirts from a different brand, or any apparel from a competing brand or professional soccer club. No jersey of any national team or club team on any subject.

---

### 5 Required Shots

1. **Two dads mid-conversation, facing the field, both in founding hats, one holding a coffee, neither looking at camera** — shot from behind at shoulder height, the field stretching to the horizon in front of them.

2. **Single dad, side profile, arms crossed, watching his kid's team warm up** — shot tight enough that the crest on his hat is readable, loose enough that his expression (focused, proud, calm) reads clearly. Morning light crossing his face from frame left.

3. **Three dads laughing at something off-frame, low-angle shot looking up from just above ground level** — the sky and top of the field lights or bleacher structure visible behind them; the moment should feel spontaneous enough that none of them are posed toward camera.

4. **A dad crouching to adjust a camp chair while his kid kicks a ball six feet away, neither looking at each other** — this is the "showing up" shot; no heroics, no performance, just presence as a habitual act.

5. **Close-up detail shot: a founding hat on a folded camp chair, a coffee cup next to it, the soccer field slightly out of focus behind** — this is the only product-forward shot in the set; it should read as still life, not product photography. Natural light only, no styling beyond placement.

---

### 2 Shot Types to Avoid

**Avoid: The "proud dad watching his kid score" reaction shot.** The dramatic celebration moment — arms up, fist pump, shouting — places the entire emotional weight of the brand on the child's performance rather than the father's presence. This brand is about showing up regardless of the score. A photo of a dad celebrating a goal implies you only matter when your kid succeeds. That is the opposite of the brand value.

**Avoid: Any shot where multiple dads are looking directly into the camera and smiling simultaneously.** This is the corporate diversity photo — it reads as auditioned, selected, and arranged. Even if the talent is perfect, coordinated eye contact with the camera signals to any viewer that this was staged for them, which collapses the authentic-community register the brand depends on. One subject making brief incidental eye contact with camera in a candid moment is acceptable. Three people posing together is not.

## 5. BRAND ASSET CHECKLIST

---

### Day 1
*(Must exist before the first Instagram post goes live)*

- **Logo package — primary crest**: SVG, PNG transparent background, PNG on Warm Cream, PNG on Charcoal. All at minimum 2000px on longest edge.
- **Logo package — wordmark-only lockup**: Same file formats. For use when the crest is too detailed (small contexts, watermarks).
- **Social avatar — platform-optimized crest crop**: 400×400px PNG, circle-safe composition confirmed. One version on Charcoal, one on Warm Cream.
- **Instagram profile grid template**: A blank 9-grid Figma or Photoshop template with the Saturday Morning palette applied, column spacing defined, and a placeholder system for how posts alternate between photography and graphic content.
- **Instagram story template set**: Three templates — announcement story, bilingual quote story, product drop teaser story. All in Saturday Morning palette, Dharma Gothic E headline, Nunito body. Delivered as Canva or Figma files.
- **Brand color hex file**: A single reference document (can be a simple one-page PDF) listing all palette colors, hex values, and usage rules. Delivered to every vendor and designer who touches the brand.
- **Founding photo set**: A minimum of 20 selects from the Day 1 shoot (see Photography Direction above), delivered as print-resolution TIFFs and social-optimized JPEGs.

---

### Pre-Drop 1
*(Must exist before the first merch drop — hats and tees go live)*

- **Hat mock-up, full 360**: Front, back, side (both), top-down. On founding Charcoal colorway. Embroidery rendering must be accurate to thread count and color — not a flat logo stamp.
- **Tee mock-up**: Front and back. On Warm Cream. Flat lay AND on-body if possible. On-body mock-up should use a real person, not a mannequin.
- **Hang tag, front and back**: Front carries the crest mark and brand name. Back carries the tagline "Every Saturday, we show up." in Dharma Gothic E and a two-line bilingual statement in Libre Franklin Italic. Tag dimensions: 2.5" × 4", printed on uncoated stock, Charcoal ink on Warm Cream paper. Die-cut with a rounded top corner and a 3mm hole for the string.
- **Email header asset**: Finalized, coded or image-exported. Warm Cream background, crest + wordmark + tagline treatment per typography spec above. Maximum 160px height. Delivered as PNG and as HTML-safe inline styling if possible.
- **Founding sticker pack, all 4 designs**: Sticker artwork finalized, print-ready at 3" minimum, 300dpi. One sticker per palette: Charcoal field, Warm Cream field, Burnt Orange field, Forest Green field.
- **Woven interior label, production file**: Delivered to the garment vendor as a production-ready embroidery file. Script mark + "SIDELINE PAPI EST. 2025" + care instructions block in Nunito.
- **Product photography, e-commerce ready**: Minimum 3 shots per SKU (founding hat, founding tee) against a Warm Cream or clean daylight background. These are not the campaign photos — these are the clean purchase-decision shots for the product page.
- **Size chart graphic**: On-brand layout, Nunito body, Dharma Gothic E headers. Not a generic size chart screenshot.

### Month 2
*(Nice to have — can be built during first active months)*

- **Hoodie mock-up, full 360**: Including interior hood lining detail shot showing Burnt Orange.
- **Chapter badge template, editable**: Circular badge format in Figma. Crest script mark in center, city/state arc at bottom, founding year at top. Color-locked to Saturday Morning palette. Designed so chapter name can be swapped without breaking the template.

Back: the crest mark and founding member language ("You were here from the start."). Uncoated stock, Charcoal ink, Warm Cream paper.
- **Press kit / media one-sheet**: For any editorial, podcast, or retail outreach. Brand story, palette reference, logo files, one approved photo. One page, PDF. Not public-facing — for partner use only.
