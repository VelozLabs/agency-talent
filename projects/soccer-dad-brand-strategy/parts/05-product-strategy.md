## 2. SUPPLIER RECOMMENDATION — Production Path

---

**Recommendation: Custom Inventory, Not POD**

POD is not the right model for this brand. The cultural constraint is explicit — no cheap blanks. Printful's Bella+Canvas 3001 and Gildan SF000 are known quantities that active apparel consumers recognize on contact. They are not wrong blanks, but they are not the blanks that earn "where did you get that?" which is the founding drop's single most important job. The Next Level 6210, Richardson 112, and IND4000 are not available through Printful or Printify at production scale. Custom inventory is the only path to the product quality this brand requires.

---

**Blank Supplier**: S&S Activewear — they stock all three blanks (Next Level, Richardson, Independent Trading Co.) under one distributor relationship, which simplifies ordering and reduces freight complexity. Account setup is straightforward for small decorators and print shops.

**Decoration Vendor**: A single local or regional decorator who can handle both screen print and embroidery in-house. This is not a category where you split vendors for Drop 1. The requirement is one shop that can run the 6210 tee (screen print), the Richardson 112 (embroidery), and the IND4000 (embroidery + screen print). Ask for a physical sample on each garment before approving the production run. If the embroidery on the Richardson 112 puckers or the Warm Cream ink on the tee looks yellow under outdoor light, the job does not go to press. Recommended approach: source two or three regional decorators, request samples from each, make the final call on sample quality, not on price.

**Minimum Order Quantities**: The Next Level 6210 and IND4000 run at one dozen (12 units) minimums per color/size at most decorators — well under the planned run. The Richardson 112 embroidery typically has a one-dozen minimum per colorway. At 75 caps per colorway, there is no MOQ problem.

**Estimated Production Timeline**: 4–5 weeks from approved artwork to finished goods in hand. Breakdown: artwork approval and separator setup (3–5 business days), blank procurement from S&S (7–10 business days), decoration production (5–7 business days), QC and pack-out (2–3 business days). Build in one week of buffer.

**Fulfillment Model**: In-house for Drop 1. At 250 units total, a 3PL is overhead the brand does not need yet. In-house fulfillment means the founder or a trusted team member physically packs and ships every founding drop order. This is not inefficiency — it is a brand signal. Founding members receive a hand-packed order. That is part of the founding drop experience. Include a hand-signed card with every order.

**3PL Transition Point (see below).**

---

**Transition Trigger**: The brand moves to a 3PL fulfillment model when any single drop exceeds 400 units or when Year 1 cumulative orders exceed 800 units total. At that volume, the in-house time cost outweighs the 3PL fee. Recommended 3PL when the time comes: ShipBob (Shopify-native integration, no long-term contract requirement, strong small-brand support) or Fulfill.com to compare regional options based on the brand's customer geography once that data exists.

The brand moves from drop-based custom inventory to a standing restock model when any single SKU sells through in under 48 hours. That signal means demand exists for continuous availability, not scarcity-model drops.

### Pricing Rationale

The core audience for Sideline Papi is the bilingual youth soccer parent who is already spending $1,500 to $4,000 per year on club fees, tournament travel, and equipment — they are not price-sensitive in absolute terms, but they are value-sensitive in relative terms. A $38 tee does not feel expensive to someone writing a $300 check for tournament registration, but it will feel wrong if the product does not deliver on that price point. That is why the blank selection is load-bearing: the Next Level 6210 at $38 justifies itself in the first wear; a Gildan Softstyle at $38 does not and it damages the brand permanently with the people it most needs to impress. The pricing is set at the ceiling of what a community product can carry without crossing into flex product territory — a $58 tee becomes a statement purchase; a $38 tee becomes a uniform. The hoodie at $78 is the same calculus: the IND4000 at 12 oz feels like a $95 product, so $78 creates the immediate perception of brand integrity. None of these prices are accessible to everyone on every sideline, which is why the brand is transparent about its production model and never discounts — scarcity-honest, quality-forward, and community-priced at the threshold where a founding member feels proud to have paid it.

The custom inventory + in-house fulfillment model for Drop 1 achieves the target margin. When the brand transitions to a 3PL at the 400-unit threshold, fulfillment costs will add approximately $3.50–$5.00 per unit, which compresses margin by 5–8 percentage points — the pricing structure has room to absorb that without repricing product, which means no sticker shock for returning customers.
