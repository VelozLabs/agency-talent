# Idea Review Template

A reusable 9-session venture review system. Point it at any new idea and get a
structured, multi-agent analysis covering market intelligence, build complexity,
UX, go-to-market, legal exposure, MVP scope, and a final go/no-go/pivot verdict.

---

## How to Use

1. **Copy this folder** and rename it for your idea:
   ```
   cp -r projects/_idea-review-template projects/your-idea-name
   ```

2. **Fill in session-00** (`session-00-idea-brief.md`) — this is the only file
   you write. Everything else is generated by the agents.

3. **Run sessions in order** (session-01 through session-08), dispatching each
   agent with the handoff brief format defined in
   `agents/product-venture-orchestrator/skills/core.md`.

4. **Update the risk register** (`risk-register.md`) after every session closes.

5. **Read the final verdict** produced by the Venture Orchestrator after
   session-08.

## Verdict Framework

After session-08 the Venture Orchestrator issues one of three verdicts:

- **GO**: No HIGH-severity open risks, credible two-sided acquisition path,
  MVP executable in ≤60 days with ≤3 people, Reality Checker finds no
  structural contradiction.

- **PIVOT**: Core thesis holds but a specific path is broken. Verdict includes
  a typed pivot (segment / channel / problem / platform) so the team knows
  exactly what to re-run.

- **NO-GO**: Structural flaw that no execution quality can overcome.

---

## Skip and Fast-Track Rules

**Skip**: The orchestrator may compress a session when uncertainty in that
domain is already resolved. Skips must be logged with rationale.

**Fast-track** (triggered after session-02 when all three apply):
1. TAM > $500M with a clear incumbent gap
2. ≥10K monthly search volume on a primary intent cluster, KD < 40
3. At least one concept with a falsifiable riskiest assumption testable in < 2 weeks

**Early kill** (triggered after session-02 or session-03):
Any structural flaw in market, build, data, or competition that no pivot resolves.
Issued as a formal document with the source agent, specific finding, and next step.
