## Round 3: Analogous Round (5 Cross-Industry Borrowings)

*What did successful platforms do in adjacent markets that could transfer here?*

1. **Thumbtack → Home Services** — Thumbtack proved local service lead gen works at scale. Orlando's home services market is huge (pool maintenance alone is a $1B+ category in FL). A vertical-focused version — AC repair, pool service, lawn care — with upfront pricing estimates and verified reviews could dominate local search.

2. **OpenTable → Local Experiences** — OpenTable solved restaurant discovery + booking in one flow. Apply the same model to Orlando's non-restaurant experience market: escape rooms, axe throwing, cooking classes, studio tours. No one owns "book a local experience in Orlando" the way OpenTable owns dinner reservations.

3. **Zillow → Rental Market** — Zillow won real estate by aggregating fragmented MLS data and adding UX. Orlando's rental market is fragmented across Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, Zillow, and dozens of local property managers. A renter-first aggregator with neighborhood filters, scam detection, and verified listings could own this.

4. **Care.com → Elderly Care** — Care.com cracked childcare matching. Orlando's retirement corridor (The Villages is 90 minutes away; many retirees live in Orange and Seminole counties) creates strong demand for in-home elder care matching that Care.com doesn't serve with local depth.

5. **Nextdoor → HOA Communities** — Nextdoor is generic. Orlando has some of the densest HOA-governed communities in the country (Celebration, Lake Nona, Baldwin Park). A community platform built specifically for HOA life — maintenance requests, vendor recommendations, meeting minutes, dispute logs — could displace Nextdoor in these neighborhoods.

## Top 3 One-Pagers

### #1 — Home Services Vertical Lead Gen

**Problem**: Orlando homeowners search "AC repair" or "pool service" and get Angi, HomeAdvisor, and Yelp — national platforms with poor local quality signals and aggressive upsells. They don't trust the results.

**Concept**: A vertical-focused lead gen platform for Orlando's highest-demand home services (AC, pool, roofing, hurricane prep) with verified local contractors, upfront pricing ranges, and real review data. Contractors pay per qualified lead.

**Differentiation**: Florida-specific needs (hurricane shutters, pool maintenance, heat pump service) that national platforms don't optimize for. Local contractor verification creates trust that Angi can't match at speed.

**Riskiest Assumption**: Contractors will pay for leads from an unproven platform before it has traffic.

**Quick Validation Path**: Run Google Ads to a landing page for "AC repair Orlando" → collect inquiries → manually match to 3 vetted contractors → charge per closed job. Validate before building anything.

---

### #2 — Orlando Rental Aggregator (Renter-First)

**Problem**: Orlando renters check Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace, and local property manager websites separately. Scams are common. Neighborhood quality signals are missing. The search experience is fragmented and stressful.

**Concept**: Aggregate all Orlando rental listings into one feed. Layer on scam detection (flagging suspicious listings), neighborhood safety scores, commute-time filters to major employers (Disney, UCF, hospital networks), and verified landlord badges. Free for renters; landlords and property managers pay for featured placement.

**Differentiation**: Renter-first UX that no aggregator currently offers. Orlando-specific data layers (flood zones, HOA restrictions, hurricane risk) as a genuine moat. Spanish-language interface from day one.

**Riskiest Assumption**: Can scrape or aggregate enough listings from disparate sources without legal friction.

**Quick Validation Path**: Manually curate 200 verified Orlando rentals, launch a simple filtered search page, measure organic traffic and renter engagement before automating data collection.

---

### #3 — Spanish-First Local Services Directory

**Problem**: Orlando has 400K+ Spanish-speaking residents. Most local service directories are English-only, making it hard to find trusted, bilingual providers for legal, medical, financial, and home services. Word-of-mouth in WhatsApp groups is the current solution.

**Concept**: A Spanish-first directory of vetted, bilingual local service providers across legal, healthcare, home repair, financial services, and immigration. Providers pay for verified listings and lead generation. Renters and homeowners get a trusted resource in their primary language.

**Differentiation**: Language-first is the moat — every competitor is English-default. Being the trusted brand for Spanish-speaking Orlando residents is a defensible position that national platforms won't optimize for.

**Riskiest Assumption**: Spanish-speaking users will use a web platform rather than relying on community referrals via WhatsApp/Facebook.

**Quick Validation Path**: Create a simple WhatsApp group or Facebook page in Spanish promoting vetted local providers. Measure engagement and inbound referral requests before building a web product.
